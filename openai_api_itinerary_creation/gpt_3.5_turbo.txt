Day 1:
Morning:
- Start your day with a visit to the Louvre Museum, home to thousands of works of art including the famous Mona Lisa.
- Lunch at Café Marly, a restaurant located inside the Louvre with a view of the museum's iconic glass pyramid.

Afternoon:
- Stroll along the Seine River and visit Notre Dame Cathedral, a masterpiece of French Gothic architecture.
- Enjoy a relaxing boat cruise on the Seine River to see the city from a different perspective.
- Dinner at Le Bistrot Paul Bert, a cozy restaurant known for its traditional French cuisine.

Day 2:
Morning:
- Explore the Montmartre neighborhood, known for its artistic history and charming streets.
- Visit the Sacré-Cœur Basilica for panoramic views of the city.
- Stop by the Musée de Montmartre, a museum dedicated to the artistic heritage of the area.

Afternoon:
- Walk through the historic Marais district and visit the Picasso Museum to see a collection of works by the famous artist.
- Indulge in a pastry tasting at local bakeries such as Boulangerie Terroirs d'Avenir or Maison Landemaine.
- Dinner at Le Procope, one of the oldest restaurants in Paris with a rich history and delicious French cuisine.

Day 3:
Morning:
- Explore the Orsay Museum, housed in a former railway station and home to an impressive collection of Impressionist and Post-Impressionist art.
- Enjoy a leisurely walk through the Tuileries Garden, a beautiful park located near the Louvre Museum.

Afternoon:
- Visit the Palace of Versailles, a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its opulent architecture and lavish gardens.
- Have a picnic in the gardens of Versailles with local delicacies such as baguettes, cheese, and wine.
- Dinner at Au Pied de Cochon, a historic brasserie open 24/7 serving classic French dishes.

Travel Tips:
- Purchase a Paris Museum Pass for access to multiple museums and skip-the-line entry.
- Take advantage of the efficient public transportation system in Paris, including the metro and buses.
- Enjoy the local cuisine by trying traditional dishes such as croissants, macarons, escargot, and coq au vin.
- Be sure to take your time to relax and enjoy the beauty and history of Paris at a leisurely pace
